Climate Change



Greenhouse gases warm the atmosphere by absorbing some of the thermal radiation released from the Earth's surface. Incoming solar radiation is transferred to the Earth's surface through the atmosphere. Instead of being transferred back to space, some of the thermal radiation is absorbed by greenhouse gases, and the atmosphere is warmed up. Due to human operations, greenhouse gas levels in the environment are growing. The major greenhouse gases directly affected by human activity are carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), nitrous oxide (N2O), chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), hydrofluorocarbons (HCFCs) and ozone (O3). Water vapor (H2O) is a major greenhouse gas as well. This website will explain the major greenhouse gases, where they come from and the impact of each on the environment.
